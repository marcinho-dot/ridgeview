## How do you start a wine collection?

Starting awine collectionmight be something you’ve thought about before. It may well be that you’re holding on to a few bottles for a special occasion. If so, congratulations: you’ve already started your wine collection journey.

The first and most important step is to get to know your palate – it’s as simple as that. You can work your way out from there. One of the great pleasures of collecting wine is the thrill of exploration and discovery. So start where you are, and build from there.

Many people find a wine they like and are happy to stick within their tastes. What sets the wine collector apart is an adventurous palate, a curious mind and some kind of emotional connection with the wines they buy.

Old vintages , esoteric gems , experimental producers and exciting new regions: if these things light a fire in you, the chances are that you’re a wine collector at heart!

## What makes a good wine collection?

There are brilliant collectible wines from just about every major wine-producing region. Whether you’re a fan of English sparkling wine or German Riesling, Rioja or Argentinian Malbec, make that your starting point and commit to premiumising.

What do we mean by premiumising? In simplest terms, it’s about buying better and drinking less (not a bad New Year’s resolution in itself!). Ageworthy wines, which should of course be a staple of your wine collection, seldom come in at less than £20 a bottle, with rare vintage wines typically costing significantly more.

So in order to kickstart yourwine collection, you will need to invest up front, buying wines to lay down and drink in future years. The commitment to drinking less comes in the knowledge that you’re saving each bottle until it’s at its peak, which may not be for a decade or more. Patience, self-discipline and the thrill of anticipation are hallmark traits of any good wine collector.

This isn’t to say that your entire collection is walled off for the future. A good wine cellar is a mix of premium wines to age and young wines ready to be drunk when the moment takes you.
