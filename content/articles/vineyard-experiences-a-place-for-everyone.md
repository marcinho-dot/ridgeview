## Vineyard Experiences – A Place for Everyone

When you think of [wine tasting](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) or [vineyard tours](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/), your mind might jump to the rolling hills of Tuscany or the cellar doors of Champagne. But world-class wine experiences are now much closer to home. England’s thriving wine scene is full of beautiful vineyards, award-winning wines, and welcoming experiences waiting to be explored.

Whether you’re a long-time lover of English wine or simply curious to discover what lies beyond the supermarket shelf, visiting a vineyard is one of the most enjoyable, and surprising, ways to spend a day in the UK. From [guided wine tastings](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) and behind-the-scenes [winery tours](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) to [long lunches with a view](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/the-rows-vine-restaurant/), English wine estates offer a fresh way to connect with nature, flavour, and a growing community of wine lovers.

## Vineyard Tours and Wine Tastings

A typical vineyard tour in England includes a guided walk through the vines, a behind-the-scenes look at how the wine is made, and a tutored tasting of selected wines (often still and sparkling) with expert insights along the way. Most tours last between 60–90 minutes and are led by knowledgeable hosts who share the story of the vineyard, the grapes, and the winemaking process.

Vineyard tours and wine tastings are one of the most enjoyable ways to explore English wine, and every estate offers something a little different. At Ridgeview, our [guided vineyard tours](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) and [wine tastings in Sussex](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) offer an inspiring glimpse at life behind the bottle – from vine to glass. Led by our expert team, they’re a brilliant way to learn more about English winemaking in a relaxed, welcoming setting.

And for those visiting without a tour booking, Ridgeview also offers something a little different: a free self-[guided vineyard trail.](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) Open to all guests, it’s a peaceful way to explore the vines at your own pace – especially when paired with a tasting flight or glass at our [vineyard restaurant](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/the-rows-vine-restaurant/), The Rows & Vine.

## Vineyard Restaurants & Dining

Dining at a vineyard is one of the most enjoyable ways to experience English wine, especially when the food is designed with the wine in mind. Across the country, winery restaurants are embracing a more seasonal, locally inspired approach, where vibrant dishes are made to be enjoyed with a glass in hand.

At Ridgeview, [The Rows & Vine](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/the-rows-vine-restaurant/) is all about letting the wine lead. Our seasonal menu is crafted to complement the flavours, structure and character of both our sparkling and still wines – from light, zesty starters to deeper, more savoury plates designed to stand up to fuller styles.

In the warmer months, guests can soak up the sunshine on our raised terrace or dine in one of our vineyard pavilions nestled by the Chardonnay vines. During winter, the restaurant moves inside the winery, offering a cosy, atmospheric setting just steps from where the wines are made.

Whether you’re pairing a tasting menu with your favourite fizz or stopping by for a glass and a few sharing plates, our [vineyard restaurant](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/the-rows-vine-restaurant/), The Rows & Vine, offers a fresh, flavourful take on wine-led dining in the heart of Sussex.

## Winery Shops & Cellar Doors

There’s something special about choosing a bottle from the place it was made. At the heart of many English vineyards is a Cellar Door – often a shop or tasting room where you can explore the wines, speak to the team, and take home something to enjoy or share.

Some offer tutored tastings or wine flights; others simply invite you to browse and ask questions. You’ll often find exclusive wines or rare vintages that aren’t available elsewhere, with people on hand who know their stuff, without ever making you feel like you need to.

At Ridgeview, our [Winery Shop in Sussex](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/visiting-us/winery-shop/) is open year-round and designed to be welcoming to all – whether you’re looking for a thoughtful gift, an exquisite food pairing or a new favourite for a special celebration. You can book a tutored [wine tasting](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/tours-and-tastings/) for a guided experience, or simply pop in for a chat to find out more about our wines and discover the bottle for you.

## Vineyard Events & Wine Club Experiences

Many English vineyards now offer events that go far beyond the glass – from harvest celebrations and wine dinners to seasonal feasts, live music, and wellness workshops.

It’s a growing side of English wine experiences, offering guests the chance to connect more deeply with the people, place, and process behind the wines.

At Ridgeview, our events calendar is closely tied to our [Wine Club](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/ourview-wine-club/) community – a space for wine lovers to gather, discover, and enjoy exclusive experiences. From new release tastings and winemaker dinners to sunset supper clubs and harvest celebrations, these one-off events bring our wines and people together in unforgettable ways.

## Vineyard Staycations in England

For those looking to stretch a day out into something longer, vineyard stays are an increasingly popular way to experience English wine. Wine GB’s directory ([find a vineyard near me](https://winegb.co.uk/visit/)) is a great place to search for vineyard accommodation across the country, while [Visit Britain](https://www.visitbritain.com/en/things-to-do/britains-best-breweries-and-vineyards) offers inspiration and resources to help plan a trip full of local highlights. For those exploring the South East, [Sussex Modern](https://sussexmodern.org.uk/) is packed with ideas for creative places to eat, stay, and discover, while [Sussex Food & Drink](https://sussexfoodanddrink.org/) showcases the region’s producers, farm shops, and food-led experiences.

If you’re looking for something extra special, Exclusive Collection’s [South Lodge](https://www.exclusive.co.uk/south-lodge/) offers luxurious spa and dining experiences just a short drive from Ridgeview. With three restaurants, a bar, lounge and terrace, it’s a peaceful base for a weekend of food, wine, and downtime. You can even book a treatment at their [Ridgeview Beauty Bar](https://www.exclusive.co.uk/south-lodge/spa/treatments/) before joining us at the estate for a tasting or tour.

At Ridgeview, while we don’t offer accommodation on-site, we’re surrounded by brilliant [places to stay in Sussex](https://www.ridgeview.co.uk/visit/visiting-us/nearby-accommodation/) and beyond. Whether you’re planning a weekend getaway, a birthday treat, or a mini-moon with a wine twist, there are options nearby to suit every style. You’ll also find excellent food and drink spots, scenic walks, cultural highlights, and independent shops in the surrounding Sussex area.

## Planning Your Visit to a Vineyard

Whether you’re looking to explore your local wine region or planning a longer getaway, visiting an English vineyard is a chance to slow down, savour the moment, and discover something new. With so many places to explore, there’s never been a better time to dive into the world of English wine.

If you’re planning your visit to Ridgeview’s vineyard in Sussex or the wider South East, these regional guides are a great place to start: [Visit Brighton](https://www.visitbrighton.com/), [Experience West Sussex](https://www.experiencewestsussex.com/), [Tourism South East](https://www.visitsoutheastengland.com/), [Visit Ditchling](https://www.visitditchling.co.uk/) and the [South Downs National Park](https://www.southdowns.gov.uk/).

To explore English wine more widely, [Wine Cellar Door](https://www.winecellardoor.co.uk/) offers a brilliant directory of vineyards open to visitors, while the Sparkling Sussex Cycling Tour combines wine tasting with scenic routes and local hospitality via the [Historic Sussex Hotels](https://www.hshotels.co.uk/).

However you choose to explore, you’ll find a warm welcome, beautiful surroundings, and plenty of reasons to raise a glass.
